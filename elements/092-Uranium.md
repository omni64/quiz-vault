# 92. Uranium (U)

**Discovered by:** Martin Heinrich Klaproth, 1789, Berlin, Germany (oxide); metal isolated 1841 by Eugène-Melchior Péligot, France

## Unique properties / facts
- Named after the planet Uranus, discovered just 8 years earlier
- U-235 fuels nuclear reactors and weapons
- Henri Becquerel's 1896 discovery of uranium's radioactivity launched nuclear physics

## Common compounds
- UO2 — reactor fuel
- UF6 — used in enrichment
- U3O8 — "yellowcake"
