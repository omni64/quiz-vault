# 72. Hafnium (Hf)

**Discovered by:** Dirk Coster & George de Hevesy, 1923, Copenhagen, Denmark

## Unique properties / facts
- Named after Hafnia, Latin for Copenhagen
- Superb neutron absorber, used in naval/nuclear reactor control rods
- HfO2 is the high-k dielectric in modern computer chip transistors

## Common compounds
- HfO2 — semiconductor dielectric
- HfC — among the most refractory ceramics known
