# 52. Tellurium (Te)

**Discovered by:** Franz-Joseph Müller von Reichenstein, 1782, Transylvania; confirmed/named by Martin Klaproth, 1798, Germany

## Unique properties / facts
- Named from Latin "tellus" (Earth) — paired conceptually with selenium (moon)
- Even trace exposure gives "tellurium breath" (garlic odor) for months
- Alloyed into steel/copper to improve machinability

## Common compounds
- Bi2Te3 — thermoelectric cooling material
- CdTe — solar cells
