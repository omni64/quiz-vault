# 41. Niobium (Nb)

**Discovered by:** Charles Hatchett, 1801, England (named "columbium"); renamed niobium by Heinrich Rose, 1844, Germany; IUPAC adopted "niobium" 1950

## Unique properties / facts
- Named after Niobe, daughter of Tantalus — reflecting its long confusion with tantalum
- Still called "columbium" in some US metallurgical usage
- Used in superconducting MRI magnets

## Common compounds
- Nb2O5
- Nb-Ti superconducting alloy wire
