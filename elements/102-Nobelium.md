# 102. Nobelium (No)

**Discovered by:** Officially credited to JINR Dubna, USSR, 1966 (earlier 1957 Stockholm and 1958 Berkeley claims were superseded, but the name was kept)

## Unique properties / facts
- Naming quirk — honors the mistaken original 1957 claimants, retained even after Dubna was credited with the real discovery
- Longest-lived isotope has a half-life of only ~58 minutes
- One of only two actinides (with Md) whose dominant solution oxidation state is +2 rather than +3

## Common compounds
- None characterized — studied only via single-atom solution tracer experiments
