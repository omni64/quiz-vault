# 114. Flerovium (Fl)

**Discovered by:** JINR Dubna, Russia with Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, USA — first synthesized 1998-99, named 2011-2012

## Unique properties / facts
- Named after the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions (Dubna), itself named for physicist Georgy Flyorov
- 2014 single-atom experiments found Fl to be a surprisingly volatile metal (comparable to mercury/astatine)
- Sits directly below lead (group 14)

## Common compounds
- None characterized in bulk — only single-atom volatility/adsorption studies
