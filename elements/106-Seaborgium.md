# 106. Seaborgium (Sg)

**Discovered by:** Albert Ghiorso et al., 1974, Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory, USA

## Unique properties / facts
- First element named after a person who was still alive (Glenn Seaborg) — controversial at the time
- Single-atom experiments produced SgO2Cl2, confirming group-6 (Mo/W) type chemistry
- Longest-lived known isotope has a half-life under a minute

## Common compounds
- SgO2Cl2 — seaborgium oxychloride, single-atom gas chemistry only
