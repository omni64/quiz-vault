# 110. Darmstadtium (Ds)

**Discovered by:** Sigurd Hofmann, Peter Armbruster et al., 1994, GSI Darmstadt, Germany

## Unique properties / facts
- Named directly after the city of Darmstadt
- Produced by fusing nickel-62 into lead-208 (cold-fusion reaction)
- Theoretically predicted to be a dense, noble-metal-like element (group 10, under Pt) — never experimentally confirmed

## Common compounds
- None characterized — too short-lived
