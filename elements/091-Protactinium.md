# 91. Protactinium (Pa)

**Discovered by:** Kasimir Fajans & Oswald Helmuth Göhring, 1913, Germany (short-lived isotope); long-lived form 1917-18 by Otto Hahn & Lise Meitner, Germany, and Frederick Soddy & John Cranston, UK

## Unique properties / facts
- Name means "parent of actinium" (decays into it)
- One of the rarest and most expensive naturally occurring elements
- Extremely toxic/radioactive with almost no practical applications

## Common compounds
- PaO2
- PaCl5 — almost nothing studied beyond trace scale
