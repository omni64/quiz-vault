# 97. Berkelium (Bk)

**Discovered by:** Glenn Seaborg, Albert Ghiorso, Stanley Thompson, 1949, Berkeley, California, USA

## Unique properties / facts
- Named directly after the city of Berkeley
- Used as target material to synthesize even heavier elements — tennessine (117) was made by bombarding Bk-249
- Has only ever been produced in microgram quantities

## Common compounds
- Bk2O3, BkCl3 — trace/research scale only
