# Country → official/native language chestnuts

- **Madagascar** — **Malagasy** (an Austronesian language, unusual for being spoken off the African coast — its closest relatives are in Borneo, reflecting the island's Southeast Asian settlers).
- **Iceland** — **Íslenska** (Icelandic), spoken in the capital **Reykjavík**; famously conservative, close to Old Norse.
- **Wales** — **Welsh**, official alongside English (a recurring "second official language of a UK nation" chestnut).
- **Kyrgyzstan** — **Kyrgyz** and **Russian** are both official.
- **Liechtenstein** and **Austria** — both officially **German**.

> [!tip] Hook
> WQC "official language of X" clues deliberately pick countries where the answer **isn't** the obvious colonial-era European language — Malagasy, not French; Kyrgyz, not just Russian.

## Cluster
- **Etymology chestnut:** "doughnut" traces to the Dutch *olykoek* ("oily cake"); many English food words derive from Dutch, French, or German loanwords — a recurring WQC Language sub-genre.
- "**Bathos**" — an alliterative-sounding term (Greek for "depth") for writing that overreaches into unintentionally comic, exaggerated sentiment.
- "**Blue blood**" (sangre azul, Spanish) is the origin of the English idiom for aristocratic lineage — visible veins on pale, unweathered aristocratic skin.
- **A stiff upper lip** — the British idiom for stoic emotional restraint.

## Mnemonic
Off-the-beaten-path official languages: **Malagasy (Madagascar), Kyrgyz (Kyrgyzstan), Welsh (Wales)** — the ones setters reach for precisely because they're surprising.
