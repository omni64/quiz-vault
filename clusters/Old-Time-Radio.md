# Old-time radio chestnuts

A thin but recurring WQC pocket: pre-TV American radio drama/comedy, plus radio-format vocabulary.

- ***Fibber McGee and Molly*** — long-running US radio sitcom; Fibber's cluttered hall closet, which spills out in an elaborate sound-effects gag every episode, became a catchphrase-level bit ("the McGee closet").
- ***The Green Hornet*** — masked crimefighter whose loyal chauffeur/crime-fighting partner is **Kato**.
- ***The Adventures of Superman*** (radio) predates the TV series and comics-to-broadcast pipeline that later defined the character on screen.
- **Shock jock** — informal term for a radio host who courts controversy/outrage for ratings (e.g. Howard Stern).

> [!tip] Hook
> Radio-era clues usually want the **sidekick or format term**, not the headline star — Kato (not the Green Hornet), the closet gag (not Fibber himself).

## Cluster
- **AM vs FM**: AM (amplitude modulation) travels farther, especially at night, but has lower audio fidelity; FM (frequency modulation) has better sound quality but shorter range — the standard technical distinction to know.
- Major US radio/TV network origins (**NBC, CBS, ABC**) all began as **radio** networks before television existed.
- **"War of the Worlds"** (1938) — Orson Welles's Mercury Theatre radio adaptation, staged as fake news bulletins, caused real (though often exaggerated in retelling) public alarm.
