# Literature — character, setting & title chestnuts

- ***To Kill a Mockingbird*** (Harper Lee) — narrator **Scout Finch**; her father, lawyer **Atticus Finch**, defends a Black man falsely accused of rape in Depression-era Alabama.
- ***My Ántonia*** (Willa Cather) — narrator Jim Burden recalls immigrant farm girl **Ántonia Shimerda** on the Nebraska prairie.
- ***Gulliver's Travels*** (Jonathan Swift) — **Lilliput** (tiny people), **Brobdingnag** (giants) — the two most-quizzed of Gulliver's four voyages.
- ***The Hunger Games*** (Suzanne Collins) is set in the nation of **Panem**, built from the ruins of North America.
- ***The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy*** (Douglas Adams) — the "____'s Guide" fill-in-the-blank format is a repeated clue trap for the full title.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Fictional place name → book" is one of the most reliable literature clue shapes: **Panem → Hunger Games, Lilliput/Brobdingnag → Gulliver's Travels, Lake Wobegon → Garrison Keillor's radio/book world.**

## Cluster
- **Philip Roth's** ***Portnoy's Complaint*** and **Umberto Eco's** ***Foucault's Pendulum*** are both classic "possessive-title-with-blank" clue fodder.
- ***Gone with the Wind*** (Margaret Mitchell) is set against the American Civil War and Reconstruction; its opening line references the antebellum South as already "gone."
- **H.G. Wells's** ***The Time Machine*** (1895) essentially invented the literary time-travel device.
- "**Purple mountain majesties**" is a line from "America the Beautiful," not a novel — a classic false-flag distractor in literature-adjacent rounds.

## Mnemonic
Fictional-place recall: **Panem = Hunger, Lilliput/Brobdingnag = Gulliver, Wobegon = Keillor.**
