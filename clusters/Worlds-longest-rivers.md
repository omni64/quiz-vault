# World's longest rivers

Quiz-standard order (mainstream / most-accepted):
1. **Nile** — ~6,650 km — Africa; drains to the **Mediterranean**.
2. **Amazon** — ~6,400 km — South America; by far the largest by **discharge/volume**.
3. **Yangtze** — ~6,300 km — longest river in **Asia** and wholly within one country (China).
4. **Mississippi–Missouri** — ~6,275 km — North America.
5. **Yenisei** — ~5,540 km — Russia/Mongolia.

> [!warning] Disputed — flag it
> **Nile vs Amazon** is genuinely contested. The traditional and quiz-accepted "longest" is the **Nile**, but several studies argue the **Amazon** is longer depending on where its source and mouth are defined. In a WQC answer, write **Nile** unless the question specifies volume (then **Amazon**).

> [!tip] Hook
> Two separate superlatives get confused: **longest = Nile**, **largest by volume = Amazon**. Setters exploit that split.

## Cluster
- **Longest in Europe:** Volga (~3,530 km) → Caspian Sea. See [[Danube]] (2nd in Europe).
- **Longest in Asia:** Yangtze. **Longest in Australia:** Murray (~2,500 km), part of the Murray–Darling system.
- **Amazon** carries roughly **20% of all river water** reaching the oceans — more than the next several rivers combined.

## Mnemonic
Top two: **N**ile is **N**umber one for **N**ame/length; **A**mazon is **A**mple for **A**mount (volume).
