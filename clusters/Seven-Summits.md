# The Seven Summits

The highest peak on each continent.

| Continent | Peak | Height | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Asia | **Everest** | 8,849 m | Highest on Earth (2020 China–Nepal joint survey; older figure 8,848 m) |
| South America | **Aconcagua** | 6,961 m | Highest outside Asia; Argentina |
| North America | **Denali** | 6,190 m | 20,310 ft — see naming flag below |
| Africa | **Kilimanjaro** | 5,895 m | Free-standing volcano, Tanzania |
| Europe | **Elbrus** | 5,642 m | Caucasus, Russia |
| Antarctica | **Vinson** (Mount Vinson) | 4,892 m | Ellsworth Mountains |
| Oceania/Australia | **disputed** | — | see below |

> [!warning] Two disputes to flag
> - **The 7th summit is contested.** The **Bass list** uses **Mount Kosciuszko** (2,228 m, Australian mainland's highest); the **Messner list** uses **Puncak Jaya / Carstensz Pyramid** (4,884 m, New Guinea — highest in Oceania). Messner's is the harder mountaineering challenge; Bass's is the "mainland Australia" answer. `#disputed`
> - **Denali** was federally renamed **Mount McKinley** by US Executive Order 14172 (Jan 2025); Alaska and international usage retain **Denali** (official in-state since 1975). For an international paper, **Denali** is the safer answer; note both. `#volatile` (as of Jul 2026)

> [!tip] Hook
> Common asks: highest in **Africa** (Kilimanjaro), **Europe** (Elbrus — *not* Mont Blanc), **Antarctica** (Vinson), and "the disputed 7th" (Kosciuszko vs Carstensz).

## Cluster
- **Mont Blanc** (4,806 m) is the highest in the **Alps** and often mis-guessed as Europe's highest — it's **Elbrus** that wins.
- **Kilimanjaro's** highest point is **Uhuru Peak** on the **Kibo** cone.
- **Aconcagua** is the highest peak in both the Southern and Western Hemispheres.
- **Denali** has the greatest **base-to-peak** rise of any land mountain (~5,500 m).

## Mnemonic
Europe trap: think **"El"**brus, not **"Mont"** Blanc — the *higher* one hides in the **Caucasus**.
