# Animal superlatives & classification traps

- **Largest animal ever (incl. extinct):** the **blue whale** — up to ~30 m, ~150–180 tonnes. Bigger than any known dinosaur.
- **Fastest land animal:** **cheetah**, ~100–120 km/h in short bursts.
- **Fastest animal overall:** **peregrine falcon**, clocked over 300 km/h in its hunting stoop (dive) — the land/air distinction is the trap in "fastest animal" questions.
- **Tallest animal:** **giraffe**. **Largest land animal:** **African bush elephant**.
- **Only mammals that lay eggs (monotremes):** the **platypus** and the **echidna** (spiny anteater) — both Australia/New Guinea only.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Fastest animal" is a two-tier trap: **land** → cheetah, **overall** → peregrine falcon (in a dive). Always listen for the qualifier, same pattern as the desert superlative trap in [[Seas-Lakes-and-Deserts]].

## Cluster
- **Marsupials** (pouched mammals — kangaroo, koala, wombat) are concentrated in **Australia**, with the **opossum** as the one common example outside it (the Americas).
- Collective nouns worth owning: a **murder** of crows, a **parliament** of owls, a **crash** of rhinos, a **pod** of whales/dolphins.
- **Octopuses have three hearts and blue blood** (copper-based haemocyanin, not iron-based haemoglobin).
- The **axolotl** is a salamander that famously **never metamorphoses**, retaining its larval gills into adulthood (neoteny) — a recurring "weird amphibian" clue.

## Mnemonic
Speed trap: **"land" cheetah, "sky/dive" falcon** — say the qualifier before the animal.
