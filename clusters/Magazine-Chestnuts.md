# Magazine chestnuts — nicknames & mottos

- ***Time*** magazine's annual **"Person of the Year"** cover is one of the most-quizzed recurring media events — a fresh answer every December, so treat any specific past winner as date-stamped, not evergreen.
- **Bon Appétit** ("BA") — French for **"enjoy your meal"**; a leading US food/culture magazine.
- **Architectural Digest** ("AD") — showcasing design and homes since **1920**.
- **Sports Illustrated** ("SI") — sections include "Leading Off" (photo) and "Scorecard" (roundup).
- ***Vogue*** ("She" is French slang shorthand in some crosswords/clues) is the flagship fashion magazine; "s'il vous plaît" wordplay is a common clue misdirection around its French-inflected branding.

> [!tip] Hook
> Magazine clues often exploit the **abbreviation** (BA, AD, SI, GQ) rather than the full title — know the shorthand, not just the name.

## Cluster
- **GQ** = *Gentlemen's Quarterly*, a men's fashion/lifestyle magazine.
- **National Geographic**, published by the National Geographic Society since 1888, is the classic "iconic yellow-bordered cover" magazine.
- **Field & Stream** ("F&S") targets hunting/fishing/outdoors readers.
- "**Pose**" as a verb (strike a model-like stance) entered mainstream slang partly via magazine/fashion-runway culture before broader pop usage.
