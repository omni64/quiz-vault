# Seas, lakes & deserts — the superlatives

Nearly every WQC ask in this sub-area is a superlative. Own the "largest/deepest/lowest" table and the traps around it.

| Superlative | Answer | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Largest lake (by area) | **Caspian Sea** | Technically an inland sea; borders **5** countries (Russia, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Iran, Azerbaijan) |
| Deepest & oldest lake | **Lake Baikal** (Siberia, Russia) | 1,642 m deep; ~25 million years old; holds ~20% of the world's unfrozen fresh water |
| Largest lake by area, freshwater only | **Lake Superior** | One of the five Great Lakes |
| Lowest point on Earth's land surface | **Dead Sea** (~430 m below sea level) | Borders Israel, Jordan & the West Bank; ~34% salinity — you float |
| Largest **hot** desert | **Sahara** (~9.2 million km²) | North Africa |
| Largest desert overall | **Antarctica** (~14 million km²) | The trap: "desert" just means low precipitation — polar counts |

> [!tip] Hook
> "World's largest desert" is a two-answer trap: **Sahara** if the setter means hot desert, **Antarctica** if they mean any desert. Listen for the qualifier.

## Cluster
- The **Aral Sea** (Kazakhstan/Uzbekistan) has shrunk to a fraction of its 1960s size after Soviet-era irrigation diversion — a standard "environmental disaster" quiz reference.
- The **Sargasso Sea** is the only sea on Earth with **no land borders**, defined instead by ocean currents (part of the North Atlantic Gyre) and named for its floating *Sargassum* seaweed; it overlaps the Bermuda Triangle.
- The **Mediterranean** is the world's best-known inland sea, linked to the Atlantic only via the narrow **Strait of Gibraltar**.
- **Lake Baikal** vs **Caspian Sea**: Baikal wins "deepest freshwater lake," Caspian wins "largest lake" outright (it's saline, sometimes classed as a sea).

## Mnemonic
Desert trap: **"hot" → Sahara, "any" → Antarctica.** Lake trap: **"biggest" → Caspian, "deepest" → Baikal.**
