# Classical music chestnuts

- A **string quartet** = **2 violins, 1 viola, 1 cello**.
- **Mozart's Symphony No. 41** is nicknamed the **"Jupiter" Symphony** — his last and grandest.
- The **Strauss family**: **Johann Strauss I** ("the Elder") and his son **Johann Strauss II** ("the Younger," "the Waltz King," composer of *"An der schönen blauen Donau"* — **"The Blue Danube"**).
- **Stravinsky** dedicated ***The Firebird*** to his teacher **Rimsky-Korsakov**.
- **Tchaikovsky's *1812 Overture*** commemorates Russia repelling Napoleon and musically quotes **"La Marseillaise"** (the French national anthem) to represent the invaders.
- **Aaron Copland** composed the score for the ballet ***Rodeo*** (and *Billy the Kid*, *Appalachian Spring*) — the sound most people associate with "American" orchestral music.

## Cluster
- **Beethoven** composed his **9th Symphony** (featuring the "Ode to Joy") while almost completely **deaf**.
- **Edvard Grieg** founded the **Norwegian Academy of Music** (1867) and is Norway's best-known composer (*Peer Gynt*, Piano Concerto in A minor).
- Fast/slow tempo markings worth owning: **allegro** (fast, lively), **andante** (walking pace), **adagio** (slow), **presto** (very fast).
- A **symphony** is for full orchestra; a **concerto** features a soloist (or small group) against the orchestra; a **sonata** is typically for one or two instruments.

## Mnemonic
String quartet head count: **"2-1-1"** — two violins, one viola, one cello.
