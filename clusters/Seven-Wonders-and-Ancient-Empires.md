# Seven Wonders & the great ancient empires

## The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
| Wonder | Location | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Great Pyramid of Giza** | Egypt | **Only survivor** — also the **oldest** of the seven |
| Hanging Gardens of Babylon | Babylon (modern Iraq) | Legendary — even its existence is disputed |
| Statue of Zeus at Olympia | Greece | Destroyed |
| Temple of Artemis at Ephesus | Ephesus (modern Turkey) | Destroyed by Goths, AD 262 |
| Mausoleum at Halicarnassus | Modern Turkey | Destroyed — origin of the word "mausoleum" |
| Colossus of Rhodes | Rhodes, Greece | Toppled by earthquake, 226 BC |
| **Lighthouse of Alexandria** (Pharos) | Egypt | Destroyed by earthquakes; guided ships **1,000+ years** |

> [!tip] Hook
> "Only surviving wonder" → **Great Pyramid of Giza**, every time. It's also the **oldest**.

## Cluster
- The **Achaemenid (Persian) Empire** — founded by **Cyrus the Great** — was ruled by **Darius I** and then his son **Xerxes I**, whose invasion of Greece was repelled at battles like **Thermopylae** and **Salamis** (480 BC).
- **Cyrus the Great** is also known for the **Cyrus Cylinder**, sometimes called an early declaration of human rights.
- Roman baths had a **caldarium** (hot room), **tepidarium** (warm room), and **frigidarium** (cold room) — a classic three-part sequence.
- **July** and **August** are the two months named for Roman rulers: **Julius Caesar** and **Augustus** respectively (August was previously "Sextilis").
- A **cornicen** was a Roman military trumpeter who signalled orders using a curved horn (the *cornu*).

## Mnemonic
Seven Wonders, one survivor: **"Giza still stands; the rest are gone."**
