# Great explorers — who did what, first

- **Christopher Columbus** styled himself **"Admiral of the Ocean Sea"** after his 1492 voyage; he never reached the North American mainland, landing instead in the Bahamas/Caribbean.
- **Ferdinand Magellan** led the expedition that achieved the **first circumnavigation of the globe** (1519–1522) — but **Magellan himself died in the Philippines** (1521); his second-in-command **Juan Sebastián Elcano** completed the voyage with the one surviving ship, *Victoria*.
- **Erik the Red** (Norse) founded the first Scandinavian settlement in **Greenland**, ~985 AD; his son **Leif Erikson** is credited as the first European to reach North America (Vinland), ~1000 AD — **before Columbus**.
- **Vitus Bering**, a Dane sailing for Russia, gave his name to the **Bering Strait** and **Bering Sea** separating Asia from North America.
- **Marco Polo** travelled to China and served the Mongol court of **Kublai Khan**; his account (*Il Milione*) introduced Europe to the wealth of the East.

> [!tip] Hook
> "First circumnavigation" is a two-name trap: the expedition is **Magellan's**, but **Elcano** is the one who actually finished it — Magellan died en route.

## Cluster
- **David Livingstone**, missing in central Africa, was found by journalist **Henry Morton Stanley** in 1871 near Lake Tanganyika, prompting the famous (possibly apocryphal) greeting **"Dr. Livingstone, I presume?"**
- **Pierre de Brazza** explored the Congo River basin for France in 1880; the capital of the Republic of the Congo, **Brazzaville**, is named after him.
- **Thor Heyerdahl** sailed the balsa-wood raft ***Kon-Tiki*** 4,300 miles across the Pacific in 1947 (101 days) to test a theory of Polynesian settlement from South America.
- **Henry Hudson**'s crew mutinied and set him adrift in **James Bay** in 1611, giving his name (posthumously) to Hudson Bay, Hudson Strait, and the Hudson River.
- **John C. Frémont**, nicknamed **"The Pathfinder,"** mapped much of the American West in the 1840s.

## Mnemonic
Circumnavigation: **"Magellan started it, Elcano finished it."**
