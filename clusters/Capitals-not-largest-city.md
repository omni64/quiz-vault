# Capitals that aren't the largest city

The classic trap: the biggest/most-famous city is **not** the capital.

| Country | Capital | Larger / better-known city |
|---|---|---|
| Australia | **Canberra** | Sydney |
| USA | **Washington, D.C.** | New York City |
| Canada | **Ottawa** | Toronto |
| Brazil | **Brasília** | São Paulo |
| Turkey | **Ankara** | Istanbul |
| New Zealand | **Wellington** | Auckland |
| Nigeria | **Abuja** | Lagos |
| Myanmar | **Naypyidaw** | Yangon |
| Switzerland | **Bern** (federal city*) | Zürich |
| Kazakhstan | **Astana** | Almaty |

> [!warning] Pedantic / volatile flags
> - **Switzerland** has *no* de jure capital; **Bern** is the "federal city." Quiz-accepted answer is still **Bern**. `#disputed`
> - **Kazakhstan's** capital was renamed **Nur-Sultan** (2019) then reverted to **Astana** (Sept 2022). Current: **Astana**. `#volatile` (as of 2026)
> - **South Africa** has **three** capitals: **Pretoria** (executive), **Cape Town** (legislative), **Bloemfontein** (judicial) — largest city is **Johannesburg**.

> [!tip] Hook
> Setters lean on Australia (Canberra), Turkey (Ankara), Brazil (Brasília) and the "purpose-built capital" angle — Canberra, Brasília, Abuja, Naypyidaw, Astana were all built or designated *to be* capitals.

## Cluster
- **Purpose-built capitals:** Canberra, Brasília, Abuja, Naypyidaw, Astana, Washington D.C. — a recurring set.
- **Vienna** on the [[Danube]] *is* both capital and largest city of Austria (contrast case).
- **Ivory Coast (Côte d'Ivoire):** official capital **Yamoussoukro**, but **Abidjan** is the seat of government and largest city.
