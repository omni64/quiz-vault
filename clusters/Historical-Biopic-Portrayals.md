# "Who played who" — historical biopic portrayals

A recurring WQC/Mastermind clue shape: name the **actor** who played a **real historical figure**, or vice versa.

| Actor | Historical figure | Film |
|---|---|---|
| **Sean Penn** | Harvey Milk (assassinated San Francisco supervisor) | *Milk* (2008) |
| **Tom Hanks** | Colonel Tom Parker (Elvis's manager) | *Elvis* (2022) |
| **Colin Firth** | King George VI | *The King's Speech* (2010) |
| **Kristen Stewart** | Diana, Princess of Wales | *Spencer* (2021) |
| **Cynthia Erivo** | Harriet Tubman | *Harriet* (2019) |
| **Forest Whitaker** | Idi Amin | *The Last King of Scotland* (2006) — won Best Actor Oscar |
| **Malcolm McDowell** | Caligula | *Caligula* (1979) |

> [!tip] Hook
> Clued either direction — "who played X" or "which real person did actor Y portray." **Forest Whitaker's Oscar-winning Idi Amin** and **Colin Firth's Oscar-winning George VI** are the two most-recycled examples of this format.

## Cluster
- **Rami Malek** won Best Actor for playing **Freddie Mercury** in *Bohemian Rhapsody* (2018); **Austin Butler** played **Elvis Presley** himself (not Parker) in *Elvis* (2022).
- **Gary Oldman** won Best Actor for playing **Winston Churchill** in *Darkest Hour* (2017).
- **Anthony Hopkins** played both **Richard Nixon** (*Nixon*, 1995) and **Pope Benedict XVI** (*The Two Popes*, 2019).
- **Cate Blanchett** has played **Queen Elizabeth I** twice (*Elizabeth*, 1998; *Elizabeth: The Golden Age*, 2007) and **Bob Dylan** in *I'm Not There* (2007).

## Mnemonic
The Oscar pairing: **Whitaker→Amin, Firth→George VI, Oldman→Churchill** — all Best Actor wins for playing a real 20th-century historical figure.
