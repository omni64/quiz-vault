# Ballet — the standard-repertoire chestnuts

- **Tchaikovsky wrote all three of the "big three" story ballets:** ***Swan Lake***, ***The Nutcracker*** (opens Christmas Eve in Dr. Stahlbaum's house), and ***The Sleeping Beauty*** (includes "The Awakening" scene).
- ***Swan Lake***: **Odette** (the White Swan, cursed princess) and **Odile** (the Black Swan, the sorcerer's daughter who deceives Prince Siegfried) are traditionally danced by the **same ballerina** — a signature technical challenge.
- ***Fancy Free*** (Jerome Robbins, score by Leonard Bernstein) follows **three sailors** on shore leave in New York City — later expanded into the musical *On the Town*.
- ***Billy the Kid*** (Aaron Copland score) is a Western-themed American ballet featuring outlaw Billy the Kid and lawman **Pat Garrett**.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Which Tchaikovsky ballet" is a three-way split — **Christmas Eve** → Nutcracker, **swans/deception** → Swan Lake, **100-year sleep/fairy godmothers** → Sleeping Beauty.

## Cluster
- Core positions/terms: **en pointe** (on the tips of the toes), **demi-pointe** (on the balls of the feet), **en couronne** (arms held curved above the head, "in a crown").
- **George Balanchine**, co-founder of New York City Ballet, is the dominant figure of 20th-century American/neoclassical ballet.
- **Rudolf Nureyev** and **Mikhail Baryshnikov** are the two most quizzed male Soviet-defector dancers of the 20th century.

## Mnemonic
Odette/Odile: **"O**dette is honest, **O**dile is a con" — but they look alike on purpose, that's the plot.
