# Social sciences — founders & core terms

- **Sigmund Freud** coined **psychoanalysis** and the **id / ego / superego** model of the psyche.
- **Émile Durkheim** is widely credited as a founder of **sociology** as an academic discipline (alongside Comte, who coined the term "sociology" itself).
- **Bronisław Malinowski** is a founding figure of modern **social anthropology**, known for fieldwork in the Trobriand Islands and pioneering participant-observation.
- **Carl Jung**, a Freud collaborator turned rival, developed **analytical psychology** and concepts like the **collective unconscious** and **archetypes**.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Founder of X social science" is a reliable clue shape — pair the discipline with its founding name (sociology→Durkheim/Comte, psychoanalysis→Freud, anthropology→Malinowski).

## Cluster
- **GDP** (Gross Domestic Product) measures output **within a country's borders**; **GNP** (Gross National Product) measures output by a country's **citizens/companies**, wherever located — the classic exam-style distinction.
- **CPI** (Consumer Price Index) tracks the cost of a basket of goods over time; it's the standard measure of **inflation**.
- Karl Marx placed **socialism** as the transitional stage **between capitalism and communism** in his historical framework.
- The **nuclear family** (two parents + children) is often cited as the basic kinship unit studied across societies, contrasted with the **extended family**.

## Mnemonic
GDP = **D**omestic (inside the border); GNP = **N**ational (by the nation's people, anywhere).
