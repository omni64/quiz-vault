# Film & TV theme chestnuts

- **"Where Everybody Knows Your Name"** — theme song of ***Cheers***, sung by **Gary Portnoy**.
- **"With a Little Help from My Friends"** — Beatles original; the version covered by **Joe Cocker** at Woodstock (1969) became forever linked with the TV show ***The Wonder Years***.
- **"Let It Go"** — sung by **Elsa** (voiced by Idina Menzel) in ***Frozen*** (2013), won the Oscar for Best Original Song.
- ***2001: A Space Odyssey*** (1968) uses **Richard Strauss's "Also sprach Zarathustra"** ("Thus Spoke Zarathustra") for its famous opening, and **Ligeti's "Lux Aeterna"** for its unsettling monolith/Stargate sequences — neither was written for the film.
- **"Viva Las Vegas"** (1964) paired **Elvis Presley** with Swedish co-star **Ann-Margret**.

> [!tip] Hook
> Film-music clues often hinge on "**this piece wasn't written for the film**" (2001's classical score) or "**who performs the Oscar-winning song, vs. who's on screen**" ("Let It Go" sung by Elsa/Menzel).

## Cluster
- **John Williams** is the most-nominated living film composer, scoring *Star Wars*, *Jaws*, *E.T.*, *Indiana Jones*, and *Harry Potter*.
- **Ennio Morricone** defined the "Spaghetti Western" sound for Sergio Leone's films (*The Good, the Bad and the Ugly*).
- **Isaac Hayes** won an Oscar for the *Shaft* (1971) theme — a landmark for Black composers in Hollywood.
- Sesame Street's opening "Sunny Day" and the Muppets' "It's Time to Play the Music" (Muppet Show theme) are classic "name that kids'-show theme" chestnuts.

## Mnemonic
2001's score: **"Sunrise fanfare = Zarathustra; monolith dread = Lux Aeterna."**
