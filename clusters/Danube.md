# Danube

- **2nd-longest river in Europe** (~2,850 km), after the [[Worlds-longest-rivers|Volga]].
- Flows through or borders **10 countries — more than any other river in the world**: Germany, Austria, Slovakia, Hungary, Croatia, Serbia, Romania, Bulgaria, Moldova, Ukraine.
- Passes **four national capitals**: [[Capitals-not-largest-city|Vienna]], **Bratislava**, **Budapest**, **Belgrade** — the most of any river.
- Rises in the **Black Forest** (Germany); empties into the **Black Sea** via the **Danube Delta**, shared by **Romania** and **Ukraine**.

> [!tip] Hook
> Clued as *"river flowing through the most countries"* or *"river passing four capitals."* The 4-capitals and 10-countries superlatives are the askable facts — own the numbers.

## Cluster
- The four Danube capitals spell a memorable west→east run down Central Europe.
- **Volga** (~3,530 km) is Europe's longest and drains to the **Caspian Sea** — pair it with the Danube as the continent's top two.
- The **Rhine–Main–Danube Canal** links the North Sea to the Black Sea across Europe.

## Mnemonic
Capitals west→east: **V**ienna, **B**ratislava, **B**udapest, **B**elgrade → "**V**ery **B**ig **B**lue **B**anube."
