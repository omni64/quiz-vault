# General history — the recurring dates

The dates that show up again and again across international quizzing, not just one country's canon.

- **1789** — Storming of the **Bastille**; start of the **French Revolution**.
- **1588** — Philip II of Spain sends the **Spanish Armada** to invade England; defeated by the English navy (and storms).
- **1914–1918** — **World War I**; ended by the **Treaty of Versailles** (signed 28 June 1919, exactly 5 years after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand that triggered the war).
- **1939–1945** — **World War II** in Europe; began with Germany's invasion of **Poland** (1 Sept 1939), ended with **VE Day** (8 May 1945) and **VJ Day** (Aug/Sept 1945).
- **1989** — Fall of the **Berlin Wall** (9 Nov), a symbolic end-point of the Cold War; Germany formally reunified in **1990**.
- **1917** — The **Russian Revolution**, bringing the Bolsheviks and Lenin to power; the last Tsar, **Nicholas II**, abdicated earlier that year.

> [!tip] Hook
> WQC "General history" rewards knowing which century-defining date a war/revolution/treaty belongs to — the exact day matters less than the year and the trigger event.

## Cluster
- **Napoleon Bonaparte** was defeated at **Waterloo** in **1815**, ending his Hundred Days return from exile on Elba.
- The **Treaty of Westphalia** (1648) ended the Thirty Years' War and is often cited as the origin of the modern nation-state system.
- **1453** — Fall of **Constantinople** to the Ottoman Turks, conventionally marking the end of the (Eastern Roman) **Byzantine Empire** and, in some textbooks, the end of the medieval period.
- **Genghis Khan** founded the **Mongol Empire** in the early 13th century, which became the largest contiguous land empire in history.

## Mnemonic
Century anchors: **1453 fall of Constantinople → 1789 French Revolution → 1917 Russian Revolution → 1945 WWII ends → 1989 Berlin Wall falls.** Five dates, five centuries of hinge points.
