# Iconic film lines → film

| Line | Film (year) |
|---|---|
| "I'll get you, my pretty, and your little dog, too!" | *The Wizard of Oz* (1939) |
| "They call me Mr. Tibbs!" | *In the Heat of the Night* (1967) |
| "I'll have what she's having" | *When Harry Met Sally...* (1989) |
| "If you build it, he will come" | *Field of Dreams* (1989) |
| "You must love me" (song) | *Evita* (1996) |

- ***Rocky*** (1976) — "his whole life was a million-to-one shot" was the film's tagline; the underdog-boxer template it set is itself a recurring reference point in quiz questions about sports films.
- ***The Legend of Bagger Vance*** — Will Smith as a mysterious golf caddie, Matt Damon as the struggling golfer.
- ***The Color of Money*** (1986) is a sequel to ***The Hustler*** (1961), both starring **Paul Newman** as pool shark "Fast Eddie" Felson — a 25-year-gap sequel, unusually long even by Hollywood standards.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Which film is this line from" clues nearly always pick a line **more famous than the film's title itself** — train on the line first, title second.

## Cluster
- **"Over the Rainbow"** and *The Wizard of Oz* (1939); **"When You Wish Upon a Star"** and *Pinocchio* (1940) — both are Best Original Song Oscar winners now more famous than their films' plots.
- ***Bringing Up Baby*** (1938) — Cary Grant and Katharine Hepburn's screwball comedy, co-starring a pet leopard named Baby.
- **Hilary Swank** won a Best Actress Oscar playing boxer Maggie Fitzgerald in ***Million Dollar Baby*** (2004).
- Distinguish this cluster's "iconic line" clues from [[Historical-Biopic-Portrayals]]'s "actor plays real person" clues — both are Film sub-area but test different recall.

## Mnemonic
Line-first recall: read the quote aloud in the actor's likely delivery — the rhythm often surfaces the title faster than thinking about plot.
