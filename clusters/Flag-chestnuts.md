# Flag chestnuts

Cheap, recurring points most competitors never drill.

- **Nepal** — the **only non-rectangular** national flag (a double-pennon / two stacked triangles).
- **Paraguay** — the **only** flag with a **different emblem on each side** (obverse vs reverse).
- **Switzerland** and **Vatican City** — the **only two square** national flags.
- **Mozambique** — the **only national flag depicting a modern firearm** (an AK-47).
- **Libya (1977–2011)** — a **plain green** field: the only **single-colour** national flag in history.
- **Denmark (Dannebrog)** — reputedly the **oldest continuously used** national flag.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Only flag that is / that has…" superlatives are the whole game here: **not rectangular** → Nepal; **two different sides** → Paraguay; **square** → Switzerland/Vatican; **a gun** → Mozambique.

## Look-alikes setters exploit
- **Indonesia** (red over white) vs **Monaco** (red over white, slightly different ratio) — near-identical.
- **Poland** is the inverse: **white over red**.
- **Chad** vs **Romania** — almost identical vertical blue-yellow-red tricolours.
- **Ireland** (green-white-orange) vs **Ivory Coast** (orange-white-green — mirrored).

## Mnemonic
The "onlys": **N**epal not-rectangular, **P**araguay two-faced, **S**wiss/**V**atican square, **M**ozambique armed. → *"No Proper Square Mozambique."*
