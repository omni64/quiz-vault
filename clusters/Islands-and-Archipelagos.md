# Islands & archipelagos

- **Greenland** is the **world's largest island** (~2.16 million km²) — **Australia is excluded by definition**, classed as a continent rather than an island (it sits alone on its own tectonic plate with a distinct flora/fauna, among other criteria).
- **New Guinea** is the 2nd-largest island; **Borneo** is 3rd, split between **three countries**: Indonesia, Malaysia, and Brunei — a reliable "which island is shared by 3 nations" answer.
- **Sweden**, not a tropical nation, has the **most islands of any country** — an estimated **267,570** (mostly tiny skerries), ahead of Norway and Finland. Only a few hundred are inhabited. Classic "most islands" trap answer.
- **Indonesia** is the world's largest **archipelagic state** by population and inhabited-island count (~17,000 islands); the **Philippines** follows with **7,641** islands.

> [!warning] Volatile — check before sitting
> **Japan** re-surveyed its coastline with modern digital mapping and in **Feb 2023** revised its official island count from **6,852 to 14,125** — more than doubling overnight purely from better measurement, not new land. `#volatile` (as of 2023 survey; current as of Jul 2026)

> [!tip] Hook
> "Country with the most islands" is a favourite trap — the intuitive tropical-archipelago answer (Indonesia, Philippines) is wrong; it's **Sweden**.

## Cluster
- Japan's four main islands: **Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, Shikoku**.
- **Madagascar** is the world's 4th-largest island and a biodiversity hotspot (>90% of its wildlife is endemic).
- **Great Britain** is the largest island in Europe; **Ireland** is the 3rd-largest in Europe after Great Britain and Iceland.
- **Baffin Island** (Canada) is the world's 5th-largest island — a common "which large Arctic Canadian island" distractor.

## Mnemonic
Size order to own: **Greenland → New Guinea → Borneo → Madagascar → Baffin.** ("Great Nations Buy Many Boats.")
