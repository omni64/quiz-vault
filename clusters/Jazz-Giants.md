# Jazz giants — birth names & signatures

WQC loves pairing a jazz legend's **stage name** with their **birth name**.

| Stage name | Birth name | Signature |
|---|---|---|
| **Dizzy Gillespie** | John Birks Gillespie | Bebop pioneer, bent trumpet bell |
| **Duke Ellington** | Edward Kennedy Ellington | Big-band composer/bandleader |
| **Sonny Rollins** | Walter Theodore Rollins | Tenor sax, "Saxophone Colossus" |
| **Chick Corea** | Armando Anthony Corea | Pianist, jazz fusion pioneer |
| **Muddy Waters** | McKinley Morganfield | Chicago blues, bridge to rock 'n' roll |
| **Bix Beiderbecke** | Leon Bismark Beiderbecke | Cornetist, died young (age 28) |

> [!tip] Hook
> "Real name of jazz legend X" is a direct, repeatable clue shape — memorise the pairs, not just the stage names.

## Cluster
- **Miles Davis**'s 1959 album ***Kind of Blue*** is the best-selling jazz album of all time.
- **Thelonious Monk** is famous for his angular, dissonant piano style and compositions like "Round Midnight."
- **Louis Armstrong**, nicknamed "Satchmo," was a foundational figure in both jazz trumpet and jazz vocals (scat singing).
- "World music" crossovers worth knowing: sitar virtuoso **Ravi Shankar** (India), and Brazilian **bossa nova** pioneers like **João Gilberto** and **Antônio Carlos Jobim**.

## Mnemonic
Birth-name trick: say the **full government name** slowly — "Edward Kennedy Ellington" *sounds* like a president, not a duke, which is exactly the misdirection setters exploit.
