# Opera — composer-to-work chestnuts

- **Mozart's last opera** is ***Die Zauberflöte*** ("The Magic Flute") — the Three Ladies give Tamino the title instrument; the villain is the Queen of the Night.
- **Puccini's *Madama Butterfly*** is set in **Nagasaki**, early 1900s — a US Navy lieutenant abandons a geisha, Cio-Cio-San.
- **Donizetti's *Lucia di Lammermoor*** is based on a Walter Scott novel and features the famous "Mad Scene."
- **Wagner's *Tristan und Isolde*** — a doomed-love opera, famous for its unresolved "Tristan chord," influential on the course of Western harmony.
- **Rossini's *The Barber of Seville*** — the barber, **Figaro**, shaves Dr. Bartolo; opens with the "Largo al factotum" aria.
- **Verdi's *Il Trovatore*** contains the **"Anvil Chorus,"** sung by gypsies literally hammering anvils onstage.
- **Gershwin's *Porgy and Bess*** is set in **Catfish Row**, a fictional Charleston, South Carolina tenement.

> [!tip] Hook
> WQC opera clues are almost always "**composer → title**" or "**setting/scene → title**" — Nagasaki is Butterfly, an anvil is Trovatore, a magic instrument is the Flute.

## Cluster
- **Offenbach's *The Tales of Hoffmann*** and **Gluck's *Orfeo ed Euridice*** are both built from myth/legend rather than a single novel source.
- Verdi and Wagner were **exact contemporaries**, both born in **1813** — a favourite "who else shares this birth year" pairing.
- **"La donna è mobile"** is from Verdi's ***Rigoletto***.
- The **Ring Cycle** (*Der Ring des Nibelungen*) is Wagner's four-opera cycle, ~15 hours performed over 4 nights.

## Mnemonic
Setting gives away the title: **anvils → Trovatore, geisha/Nagasaki → Butterfly, magic flute → Zauberflöte, barber → Barber of Seville.**
