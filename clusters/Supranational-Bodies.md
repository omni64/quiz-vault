# Supranational bodies — membership counts

Membership counts are the single most-asked fact type here, and they **change**. All figures below are current as of **July 2026** — re-verify before a sitting.

| Body | Members | Founded | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **United Nations** | **193** member states | 1945 | HQ New York; 5 permanent, veto-holding Security Council members: **US, UK, France, Russia, China** |
| **European Union** | **27** | 1993 (Maastricht Treaty); precursor EEC 1957 | Dropped from 28 after **Brexit**, Jan 2020 |
| **Eurozone** (EU members using the euro) | **21** | Euro launched 1999 | **Bulgaria** became the 21st euro-area member **1 Jan 2026**; Denmark, Sweden, Poland, Czechia, Hungary, Romania remain outside |
| **NATO** | **32** | 1949 | **Finland** joined 2023 (31st), **Sweden** 2024 (32nd) — both post-dating Russia's invasion of Ukraine |
| **ASEAN** | **11** | 1967 | **Timor-Leste** became the 11th member **26 Oct 2025** — the first new member in 26 years |
| **BRICS** | **11** full members | Founded 2009 (as BRIC); renamed 2011 | Original 5 (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa) expanded **2024–2026** to add Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia |

> [!warning] These are the most volatile facts in the whole vault
> ASEAN, BRICS and Eurozone membership have each changed within the last 8 months of writing. Re-check all six rows above close to your sitting date. `#volatile` (as of Jul 2026)

> [!tip] Hook
> WQC "current affairs" and "world affairs" clues love a fresh accession — the most recently admitted member of any bloc is disproportionately likely to be asked.

## Cluster
- The euro is also used **outside** the EU/eurozone by agreement or unilaterally: **Andorra, Monaco, San Marino, Vatican City** (agreements) and **Kosovo, Montenegro** (unilateral, no formal agreement).
- **Switzerland** is in neither the EU nor the eurozone, despite being geographically surrounded by both.
- Non-EU eurozone members currently number **zero** by treaty — but see above for unilateral/agreement users.
- **G7** (US, UK, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Canada, + EU) is a forum, not a formal organisation with a treaty or HQ — a useful contrast with NATO/EU/UN.

## Mnemonic
Newest-member chestnuts: **Timor-Leste → ASEAN (2025)**, **Bulgaria → Eurozone (2026)**, **Sweden → NATO (2024)** — "the last one in" is the question setters reach for.
