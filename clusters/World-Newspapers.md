# World newspapers — name-means-what & who's-where

- **Le Monde** (France) — literally **"The World."**
- **O Globo** (Brazil) — **"The Globe."**
- **Chosun Ilbo** (South Korea) — one of the country's oldest and largest daily papers.
- **Amar Ujala** (India, Hindi-language) — the name translates roughly to **"immortal light."**
- **Magyar Hírlap** (Hungary) — "Hungarian Herald/News."

> [!tip] Hook
> "This newspaper's name literally means ___" is the standard clue shape — **Le Monde = The World** is the most-recycled example.

## Cluster
- **The Wall Street Journal** and **The New York Times** are the two highest-circulation quality US dailies; internationally, **The Times** (London, founded 1785) is one of the oldest continuously published newspapers.
- **Wire services** — **Associated Press (AP)** and **Reuters** — supply syndicated news to outlets worldwide rather than publishing under their own masthead primarily.
- The **Pulitzer Prize** (est. 1917, endowed by Joseph Pulitzer) is US journalism and letters' top honour; the **Pulitzer Prize for Public Service** is its most prestigious journalism category.
- A photo **caption** (also called a "cutline") is the short explanatory text beneath a news photograph or graphic.

## Mnemonic
Translate-the-name trick: **Le Monde = World, O Globo = Globe** — foreign-paper names are quizzed precisely because they translate to something memorable in English.
