# Plant & tree chestnuts

- **Tallest tree species:** **coast redwood** (*Sequoia sempervirens*), California — the tallest known individual, "Hyperion," is over 115 m.
- **Oldest known individual (non-clonal) tree:** a **Great Basin bristlecone pine** in California, nicknamed "Methuselah," at roughly **4,850 years old**. (The record-holder's exact location is kept secret to protect it.)
- **Oldest clonal organism:** **Pando**, a single quaking-aspen root system in Utah, estimated at **thousands to ~80,000 years** old by some estimates — one genetic individual covering ~43 hectares.
- **Largest carnivorous plant genus by fame:** *Nepenthes* (tropical pitcher plants); the best-known carnivorous plant overall is the **Venus flytrap**, native only to a small area of North/South Carolina.

> [!tip] Hook
> "Tallest/oldest tree" questions almost always want the **coast redwood** (tallest) or a **bristlecone pine** (oldest) — own those two species names cold.

## Cluster
- The **moccasin flower** is another name for the **pink lady's slipper**, a North American wild orchid — a recurring "flower's other name" clue.
- **Photosynthesis** converts CO₂ + water + light into glucose + oxygen; the green pigment doing the work is **chlorophyll**.
- A flower's **stamen** is the male part (produces pollen); the **pistil/carpel** is the female part.
- **Bamboo** is technically a **grass**, not a tree, and is among the fastest-growing plants on Earth.

## Mnemonic
Tallest vs oldest: **"tall" → redwood, "old" → bristlecone pine.**
